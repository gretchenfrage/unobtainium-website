OUR PROJECT

We are developing a system for guided landing via a dynamically reefed parachute. The system will activate at apogee, and a camera will began analysing the ground for an optimal place to land. An on-board computer will then calculate the optimal configuration for the parachute, and wirelessly transmit it to a controller device.

The parachute controller will adjust the coords of the parachute (reefing), changing the rocket's velocity, and causing it to land in a desired location. Landing site selection will be based on colors and patterns, to simulate extraterrestrial mining operations.





THE VEHICLE

We will use a single stage, J-class vehicle to reach a target altitude of one mile. The vehicle will be constructed from filament wound fiberglass tubing and CNC cut fiber glass fins. The rocket will endure at least 25g of acceleration, and the stress of high powered flight and deployment.

The rocket will be 62" long, 3" in diameter, and weigh 11.2lbs. It will have a total impulse of 1229Ns, giving it a thrust:weight ratio of 20.09.
